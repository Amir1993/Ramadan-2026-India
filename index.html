🌙 SEHRI NIYYAH (ROZA KI NIYYAT)
نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أَصُومَ صَوْمَ غَدٍ مِنْ شَهْرِ رَمَضَانَ الْمُبَارَكِ فَرْضًا لَكَ يَا اللَّهُ فَتَقَبَّلْ مِنِّي إِنَّكَ أَنتَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Nawaitu an asooma sawma ghadin min shahri Ramadaanil Mubaarak · fardal-laka yaa Allah · fataqabbal minni · innaka antas Samee'ul 'Aleem
"I intend to observe the fast tomorrow in the blessed month of Ramadan as an obligatory act of worship for You, O Allah. Accept it from me, indeed You are the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing."
— Standard Hanafi Niyyah · India & Pakistan
☀️ IFTAR DUA (ROZA KHOLNE KI DUA)
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي لَكَ صُمْتُ وَبِكَ آمَنْتُ وَعَلَيْكَ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَعَلَى رِزْقِكَ أَفْطَرْتُ فَتَقَبَّلْ مِنِّي
Allahumma inni laka sumtu · wa bika aamantu · wa 'alayka tawakkaltu · wa 'alaa rizqika aftartu · fataqabbal minni
"O Allah! I fasted for You, I believe in You, I placed my trust in You, and I break my fast with Your provision. Accept it from me."
— Abu Dawud 2358 · Authenticated (Full Version)
💧 DUA AFTER BREAKING FAST (THIRST GONE)

Dhahaba az-zama'u · wabtallatil 'urooqu · wa thabatal ajru · in sha Allah
"The thirst has gone, the veins are moistened, and the reward is confirmed — if Allah wills."
— Abu Dawud 2357 · Hasan Sahih
🌄 WAKING UP (NEEND SE UTHNE KI DUA)
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي أَحْيَانَا بَعْدَ مَا أَمَاتَنَا وَإِلَيْهِ النُّشُورُ
Alhamdu lillahil-ladhi ahyaanaa ba'da maa amaatanaa wa ilayhin nushoor
"All praise is for Allah who gave us life after causing us to die, and to Him we will return."
— Bukhari 6312
😴 BEFORE SLEEPING (SONE KI DUA)
اللَّهُمَّ بِاسْمِكَ أَمُوتُ وَأَحْيَا
Allahumma bismika amootu wa ahyaa
"O Allah, in Your name I die and I live."
— Bukhari 6312 · Muslim 2711
🌅 MORNING DUA (SUBAH KI DUA)
اللَّهُمَّ بِكَ أَصْبَحْنَا وَبِكَ أَمْسَيْنَا وَبِكَ نَحْيَا وَبِكَ نَمُوتُ وَإِلَيْكَ النُّشُورُ
Allahumma bika asbahnaa · wa bika amsaynaa · wa bika nahyaa · wa bika namootu · wa ilayka nushoor
"O Allah, by Your leave we have reached the morning, by Your leave we reach the evening, by Your leave we live, by Your leave we die, and to You is the resurrection."
— Abu Dawud 5068 · Tirmidhi 3391
🌙 EVENING DUA (SHAM KI DUA)
اللَّهُمَّ بِكَ أَمْسَيْنَا وَبِكَ أَصْبَحْنَا وَبِكَ نَحْيَا وَبِكَ نَمُوتُ وَإِلَيْكَ الْمَصِيرُ
Allahumma bika amsaynaa · wa bika asbahnaa · wa bika nahyaa · wa bika namootu · wa ilayal maseer
"O Allah, by Your leave we reach the evening, by Your leave we reach the morning, by Your leave we live, by Your leave we die, and to You is the final return."
— Abu Dawud 5068 · Tirmidhi 3391
🍽️ BEFORE EATING (KHANE SE PEHLE)

"In the name of Allah and with the blessings of Allah."
— Abu Dawud 3767 · Ibn Majah 3264
🙏 AFTER EATING (KHANE KE BAAD)
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي أَطْعَمَنَا وَسَقَانَا وَجَعَلَنَا مِنَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ

"All praise is for Allah who fed us and gave us drink, and made us Muslims."
— Abu Dawud 3850 · Tirmidhi 3457
🚪 LEAVING HOME (GHAR SE NIKALNE KI DUA)
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ وَلَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ
Bismillahi tawakkaltu 'alallahi · wa laa hawla wa laa quwwata illaa billaah
"In the name of Allah, I place my trust in Allah. There is no power and no strength except with Allah."
— Abu Dawud 5095 · Tirmidhi 3426 · Sahih
🏠 ENTERING HOME (GHAR MEIN DAKHIL HOTE WAQT)
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ خَيْرَ الْمَوْلِجِ وَخَيْرَ الْمَخْرَجِ بِسْمِ اللَّهِ وَلَجْنَا وَبِسْمِ اللَّهِ خَرَجْنَا وَعَلَى اللَّهِ رَبِّنَا تَوَكَّلْنَا
Allahumma inni as'aluka khayral mawliji wa khayral makhraji · bismillahi walajna wa bismillahi kharajna · wa 'alallahi rabbinaa tawakkalnaa
"O Allah, I ask You for the best entry and the best exit. In the name of Allah we enter, and in the name of Allah we leave, and upon Allah our Lord do we rely."
— Abu Dawud 5096
🚿 ENTERING BATHROOM (WAQT DAAKHIL HONE KI DUA)
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْخُبُثِ وَالْخَبَائِثِ
Allahumma inni a'oodhu bika minal khubthi wal khabaa'ith
"O Allah, I seek refuge with You from the male and female evil spirits."
— Bukhari 142 · Muslim 375
🪞 LOOKING IN MIRROR (AAINE MEIN DEKHNE KI DUA)

📿 SAYYIDUL ISTIGHFAR (MASTER DUA OF FORGIVENESS)
اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allahumma anta rabbi · laa ilaaha illaa anta · khalaqtani wa ana 'abduka · wa ana 'alaa 'ahdika wa wa'dika mastata'tu · a'oodhu bika min sharri maa sana'tu · aboo'u laka bi ni'matika 'alayya · wa aboo'u bi dhambi · faghfir li · fa innahu laa yaghfirudh dhunooba illaa ant

I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour upon me and I acknowledge my sin, so forgive me, for truly none forgives sins but You."
— Bukhari 6306 · Whoever says this with firm belief and dies that day before evening enters Jannah

🌙 ROZA KI NIYYAT (SEHRI NIYYAH) · نیت روزہ
RECITE BEFORE SEHRI ENDS · HANAFI (INDIAN) PRACTICE
نَوَيْتُ أَنْ أَصُومَ صَوْمَ غَدٍ مِنْ شَهْرِ رَمَضَانَ الْمُبَارَكِ فَرْضًا لَكَ يَا اللَّهُ فَتَقَبَّلْ مِنِّي إِنَّكَ أَنتَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Nawaitu an asooma sawma ghadin · min shahri Ramadaanil Mubaarak · fardal-laka yaa Allah · fataqabbal minni · innaka antas Samee'ul 'Aleem
"I intend to observe the fast tomorrow in the blessed month of Ramadan as an obligatory act for You, O Allah. Accept it from me — indeed You are the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing." — Standard Hanafi Niyyah · India & Pakistan
🌙 ROZA KI NIYYAT — SHORT VERSION
ALSO VALID · SIMPLE INTENTION
اللَّهُمَّ أَصُومُ غَدًا لَكَ فَتَقَبَّلْ مِنِّي
Allahumma asoumu ghadan laka fataqabbal minni
"O Allah, I fast tomorrow for You, so accept it from me." — A valid short niyyah. Remember: the Niyyah is primarily an intention of the heart, not just words. Both versions are acceptable.

📖 SURAH AL-KAHF — LAST 10 AYAAT (18:101-110)
LIGHT BETWEEN YOU & DAJJAL ON JUDGMENT DAY
١٠١. الَّذِينَ كَانَتْ أَعْيُنُهُمْ فِي غِطَاءٍ عَن ذِكْرِي وَكَانُوا لَا يَسْتَطِيعُونَ سَمْعًا
١٠٢. أَفَحَسِبَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا أَن يَتَّخِذُوا عِبَادِي مِن دُونِي أَوْلِيَاءَ ۚ إِنَّا أَعْتَدْنَا جَهَنَّمَ لِلْكَافِرِينَ نُزُلًا
١٠٣. قُلْ هَلْ نُنَبِّئُكُم بِالْأَخْسَرِينَ أَعْمَالًا
١٠٤. الَّذِينَ ضَلَّ سَعْيُهُمْ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَهُمْ يَحْسَبُونَ أَنَّهُمْ يُحْسِنُونَ صُنْعًا
١٠٥. أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِآيَاتِ رَبِّهِمْ وَلِقَائِهِ فَحَبِطَتْ أَعْمَالُهُمْ فَلَا نُقِيمُ لَهُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ وَزْنًا
١٠٦. ذَٰلِكَ جَزَاؤُهُمْ جَهَنَّمُ بِمَا كَفَرُوا وَاتَّخَذُوا آيَاتِي وَرُسُلِي هُزُوًا
١٠٧. إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ كَانَتْ لَهُمْ جَنَّاتُ الْفِرْدَوْسِ نُزُلًا
١٠٨. خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا لَا يَبْغُونَ عَنْهَا حِوَلًا
١٠٩. قُل لَّوْ كَانَ الْبَحْرُ مِدَادًا لِّكَلِمَاتِ رَبِّي لَنَفِدَ الْبَحْرُ قَبْلَ أَن تَنفَدَ كَلِمَاتُ رَبِّي وَلَوْ جِئْنَا بِمِثْلِهِ مَدَدًا

🛡️ PROTECTION FROM THE GRAVE — AL-MANI'AH
The Prophet ﷺ named it “Al-Mani'ah” (The Preventer) because it prevents the punishment of the grave. A man who recited it will be interceded for until all his sins are forgiven. The surah argues for him like a lawyer before Allah.
Tirmidhi 2891 · Abu Dawud 1400 · Ibn Majah 3786 · Authenticated
🌙 THE PROPHET ﷺ NEVER SLEPT WITHOUT IT
Jabir (RA) narrated: “The Prophet ﷺ would not sleep until he recited Surah As-Sajdah and Surah Al-Mulk.” This was a consistent Sunnah every single night without exception — make it yours too in this Ramadan.
Ahmad 14715 · Tirmidhi 2892 · Authentic chain
📣 INTERCEDES ON YAWM AL-QIYAMAH
On the Day of Judgement, Surah Al-Mulk will appear as an advocate and argue in your defence before Allah — shielding you from the Fire. It intercedes until its companion is forgiven. No other deed may match the constancy of this one nightly habit.
Abu Dawud 1400 · Albani authenticated · Hakim 2/498
💜 PAIR WITH SURAH SAJDAH (32) — THE SUNNAH PAIR
Recite both together nightly: Surah As-Sajdah (32) then Surah Al-Mulk (67). Together they bring protection from grave punishment, intercession on Judgement Day, and noor between Isha and Fajr. Among the greatest consistent worship a Muslim can maintain.
Tirmidhi 2892 · Sahih chain · Ibn Rajab al-Hanbali
🌟 THE FIRST NIGHT IN THE GRAVE
The night of burial is the most frightening night a soul will ever face. Scholars said Surah Al-Mulk shields the believer in Barzakh — the angels of punishment are turned away and cannot reach the one who recited it faithfully. Make sure your last words in this world include Surah Al-Mulk.
Ibn Kathir, Tafseer Surah Al-Mulk · Hakim 2/498

AFTER UMRAH IN RAMADAN 🌙
Your Umrah is complete — but Ramadan in Makkah offers incredible additional worship opportunities. Make the most of every moment:
🕌 TARAWEEH IN MASJID AL-HARAM
Praying Taraweeh in Masjid Al-Haram earns 100,000× the reward of praying elsewhere. The Imams complete the entire Quran in Taraweeh every Ramadan. Arrive early for spots near the Kaaba.
📖 QURAN KHATAM IN HARAM
Sit near the Kaaba and recite Quran as much as possible. Every letter in Haram earns 100,000 hasanaat. Aim to complete at least one Khatam during your stay.
🌟 LAYLATUL QADR — LAST 10 NIGHTS
Worship in Laylatul Qadr = 1,000 months (83+ years) of worship. Odd nights (21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, 29th Ramadan) are most likely. Spend the night in I'tikaf, Tawaf, and du'a.
🔄 PERFORM ANOTHER UMRAH
Go to Masjid Aisha (Tan'eem) by taxi (~20 min), enter Ihram, return for another Tawaf + Sa'i. Every extra Umrah in Ramadan earns Hajj-equivalent reward — inshallah maqbool!
🤲 IFTAR AT HARAM
Free Iftar spread inside Haram — arrive 30 min early. Sit anywhere in the courtyard and break fast with Zamzam and dates alongside millions of Muslims. Truly unforgettable.

THE SACRED BOUNDARIES
MIQAT · المواقيت
The sacred stations where every pilgrim must enter Ihram before approaching Makkah
🌍 THE FIVE CLASSIC MIQAT STATIONS
🇮🇳 YALAMLAM · يلملم (As-Sa'diyah)
For pilgrims from India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Yemen and South Asia. Located ~100 km south of Makkah. Most Indian flights pass over this Miqat — wear Ihram on the plane before this point or at the airport!
DHUL HULAYFAH · ذو الحليفة (Abyar Ali)
For pilgrims coming from Madinah. The farthest Miqat (~450 km from Makkah). If visiting Madinah first, enter Ihram here at Masjid Ash-Shajarah before departing to Makkah.
AL-JUHFAH · الجحفة (Rabigh)
For pilgrims from Egypt, Syria, Morocco, Algeria, West Africa. ~204 km northwest of Makkah. Masjid Ar-Rabitah is built at this location today.
QARN AL-MANAZIL · قرن المنازل (As-Sayl Al-Kabeer)
For pilgrims from Najd, Riyadh, and UAE. ~94 km east of Makkah. Also called "As-Sayl" — a large mosque marks this Miqat today. Easily accessible from Taif highway.
DHAT IRQ · ذات عرق
For pilgrims from Iraq, Iran, Kuwait and east of the Gulf. ~94 km northeast of Makkah. This Miqat was set by Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA) based on the Prophet's ﷺ direction.
🟢 ALREADY INSIDE SAUDI ARABIA
If you are already in Makkah (e.g. performing a 2nd Umrah, or a Saudi resident), you cannot enter Ihram from inside Makkah — you must go to one of these near-Makkah Miqat points:
🕌 MASJID AISHA · مسجد عائشة (Tan'eem)
~7 km
The nearest Miqat from Makkah, located in the Tan'eem district. Named after Aisha (RA) because the Prophet ﷺ sent Abdur-Rahman (RA) with her here to put on Ihram for a separate Umrah after Hajj. This is the most popular place for performing additional Umrahs during Ramadan. Taxis and buses available 24/7 from Haram.
⭐ BEST CHOICE FOR 2ND UMRAH IN RAMADAN
🕌 MASJID JI'IRRANAH · مسجد الجعرانة
~26 km
Located northeast of Makkah toward Taif. This is where the Prophet ﷺ himself entered Ihram for Umrah after the Ghazwah of Hunayn (8 AH). A Sunnah Miqat of special significance. Performing Umrah with Ihram from Ji'irranah is especially recommended.
🌟 THE PROPHET ﷺ PERSONALLY USED THIS MIQAT
🕌 HUDAYBIYYAH · الحديبية (Ash-Shumaisi)
~22 km
Located west of Makkah toward Jeddah. Famous as the site of the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (6 AH) — one of the most significant events in Islamic history, where Allah revealed Surah Al-Fath. A small mosque marks the site. Third Miqat option for those already inside Saudi.
🏔️ TAIF ROUTE → QARN AL-MANAZIL
~90 km
If you are visiting or staying in Taif (a beautiful hill-station ~90 km from Makkah), the Miqat for you is Qarn al-Manazil (As-Sayl Al-Kabeer) on the Taif-Makkah highway. A grand mosque with full facilities marks this spot. Enter Ihram here before descending toward Makkah. Taif is known for roses, honey, and pleasant weather — many Saudis vacation here during Ramadan.
🏙️ IF STAYING IN JEDDAH
Jeddah itself is inside the Miqat boundary. Do NOT enter Makkah from Jeddah without Ihram. Your options are: (1) Enter Ihram at your Jeddah hotel before leaving for Makkah — as you will pass Miqat on the way; OR (2) Go to Hudaybiyyah or Tan'eem specifically to enter Ihram. The second Umrah from Jeddah: go first to Tan'eem, enter Ihram, then proceed to Makkah.
⚠️ CRITICAL — INDIANS VIA JEDDAH AIRPORT
The flight from India crosses the Yalamlam Miqat in the air. You MUST already be in Ihram at this point. Wear Ihram before boarding, or at the Mumbai/Delhi/Hyderabad airport. If you land in Jeddah without Ihram, you must wear it at the airport before leaving for Makkah — otherwise a penalty sacrifice (Dam) is required.
🕋 MAKKAH
💚 MADINAH
Makkah al-Mukarramah — the holiest city on earth. Every step here carries immense reward. Spend as much time as possible in Tawaf and ibadah.
🕌 INSIDE MASJID AL-HARAM
🕋
The Holy Kaaba · الكعبة المشرفة
The House of Allah — the direction of all Muslims' prayers. Perform Tawaf (7 rounds anti-clockwise). Try to touch the Kaaba's wall or Multazam (between Black Stone and door) and make heartfelt dua — this spot is known as a place where duas are accepted. The golden door (Bab al-Kaaba) faces North-East.
📍 MAP ↗
🖤
Hajar al-Aswad · الحجر الأسود
The Black Stone — descended from Jannah. Set in the east corner of the Kaaba. Kiss it if possible or point to it at the start of each Tawaf circuit. The Prophet ﷺ said it will testify for those who kissed it on the Day of Judgement. Best time to kiss: late night after Tahajjud prayers (least crowd).
🟡
Maqam Ibrahim · مقام إبراهيم
The stone bearing the footprint of Ibrahim (AS) — where he stood to call people to Hajj. After completing Tawaf, pray 2 Rakats here with Maqam Ibrahim between you and the Kaaba. Read Surah Kafirun (109) in 1st rakat, Surah Ikhlas (112) in 2nd.
💧
Zamzam Well · بئر زمزم
The blessed well that has flowed for over 4,000 years — since Hajra (AS) ran between Safa and Marwah for water for baby Ismail (AS). Drink facing the Kaaba, in 3 breaths. Make a sincere dua while drinking — Zamzam is for whatever you intend (shifa, rizq, knowledge, Jannah). The underground drinking hall is accessible from inside the Haram.
🏃
Safa & Marwah · الصفا والمروة
The two hills between which Hajra (AS) ran 7 times in search of water for Ismail (AS). Now enclosed in the Mas'aa corridor attached to the Haram (east side). Sa'i = walking 7 times between them (Safa→Marwah = 1 trip). Men jog in the green-lit section. Make personal dua throughout — no specific wording required between hills. End at Marwah.
🌙
Hateem / Hijr Ismail · الحِجر
The semicircular wall on the north side of the Kaaba — this area is actually part of the original Kaaba that Ibrahim (AS) built. Praying 2 Rakats inside the Hateem is equivalent to praying inside the Kaaba itself. Walk around (not through) it during Tawaf. Very special place for dua — scholars consider this one of the most powerful places on earth for supplication.
🚪
Multazam · الملتزم
The section of the Kaaba wall between the Black Stone and the Kaaba door — literally means "the place to cling to." Press your chest, cheek and hands against this wall and pour out your heart in dua. The Prophet ﷺ used to make dua here. One of the most powerful spots in the world for accepted supplications.
🪟
Rukn al-Yamani · الركن اليماني
The Yemeni Corner — the south-west corner of the Kaaba. Touch it with your right hand (or both hands) during each Tawaf circuit if possible — it is an authenticated Sunnah. Do NOT point to it like the Black Stone — only touch. Between here and the Black Stone, recite Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan (Quran 2:201).
🏔️ AROUND MAKKAH
🌄
Jabal al-Nour · جبل النور (Cave Hira)
The Mountain of Light — containing Cave Hira (Ghar Hira) where the first revelation of the Quran was revealed to the Prophet ﷺ (Surah Alaq 96:1). A steep 1-hour climb with ~1,500 steps. Start before sunrise to avoid extreme heat. Take water. The cave is small — about 1.5m wide. Make dua and recite Quran inside. Located ~4km NE of Haram. Allah's name was first uttered from this mountain. 🏔️ Distance: ~4km from Haram.
📍 MAP ↗
🏔️
Jabal Thawr · جبل ثور (Cave Thawr)
The cave where the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr (RA) hid for 3 days during the Hijrah migration to Madinah. Allah sent a spider's web and a dove's nest to conceal them from the Quraysh. Mentioned in the Quran (9:40). A 1.5–2 hour climb, more difficult than Hira. Located ~6km south of the Haram.
📍 MAP ↗
🪦
Jannat al-Mu'alla · جنة المعلى
The ancient Makkah cemetery where the family of the Prophet ﷺ is buried — including Khadijah (RA) his first wife, Abdul Muttalib his grandfather, Aaminah his mother (some scholars say), and Qasim and Abdullah his sons. Make salaam and recite Surah Fatiha and dua for those buried here. Open for visitation — check current timings as they vary. ~500m north of the Haram.
📍 MAP ↗
🕌
Masjid al-Jinn · مسجد الجن
Built at the spot where a group of Jinn heard the Prophet ﷺ recite the Quran and accepted Islam — as mentioned in Surah al-Jinn (72:1). A small historical mosque ~1km NE of the Haram. Pray 2 Rakats and reflect on the universality of Islam — even the Jinn submitted to Allah upon hearing His words.
📍 MAP ↗
🏠
Birthplace of Prophet ﷺ · مولد النبي
The area in the Suq al-Layl district where the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in 570 CE. Now converted into a library (Maktabah Makkah al-Mukarramah). Located in the old city ~500m from the Haram. Stand here and recite abundant Salawat on the Prophet ﷺ and reflect on the greatest blessing to mankind.
📍 MAP ↗
🕌
Masjid Aisha / Tan'eem · مسجد عائشة
The nearest Miqat from Makkah — 7.5km north of the Haram. Named after Aisha (RA) who put on Ihram from here by the Prophet's ﷺ instruction. Visit here for a second or third Umrah during your stay. Every Umrah is a fresh spiritual cleansing. Take a taxi (~10 min), wear Ihram, make niyyah, return and perform Tawaf + Sa'i + Halq again. Inshallah Maqbool!
📍 MAP ↗
🏟️
Mina · منى
The valley of tents 8km east of Makkah — used primarily during Hajj for pilgrims to camp and perform the stoning of the Jamarat (Shaytaan). For Umrah visitors, visit Masjid al-Khayf here — where the Prophet ﷺ prayed many times and 70 prophets before him. Also see the Jamarat Bridge — three pillars symbolising where Ibrahim (AS) stoned Shaytaan.
📍 MAP ↗
🌄
Arafat · عرفات
The plain of Arafat — the most important site of Hajj ("Hajj is Arafat"). The Prophet ﷺ delivered his final sermon here on Jabal ar-Rahmah (Mount of Mercy). The Day of Arafat (9th Dhul Hijjah) is when Allah descends and forgives. For Umrah visitors: visit the mount, make abundant dua and istighfar. Located ~20km east of Makkah.
📍 MAP ↗
🌃
Muzdalifah · مزدلفة
The open plain between Arafat and Mina where Hajj pilgrims spend the night under the stars. Contains Masjid al-Mash'ar al-Haram (Sacred Monument Mosque) mentioned in the Quran (2:198). For Umrah visitors: pass through during a day trip to Arafat and reflect on Hajj.
🏗️
Abraj Al-Bait & Makkah Museum
The iconic Makkah Clock Tower (world's 3rd tallest building) directly overlooks the Haram. Take the observation deck for a stunning aerial view of the Masjid al-Haram and the Kaaba. The Royal Makkah Clock Tower Museum (floor 4) has excellent displays on Islamic history, Hajj, and the Kaaba's construction. The mall below has pharmacies, restaurants and prayer supplies.

AL-MADINAH AL-MUNAWWARAH · المدينة المنورة
"The City of the Prophet ﷺ" — the city whose every stone is blessed. Every salah in Masjid Nabawi earns the reward of 1,000 salah elsewhere (except Masjid al-Haram). The Prophet ﷺ said: "He who is able to die in Madinah, let him die there."
Spend as much time here as possible · Every step is sadqa

💚 MASJID AN-NABAWI
💚
Masjid an-Nabawi · المسجد النبوي
The Prophet's ﷺ Mosque — the second holiest masjid in Islam. Every salah here = 1,000 salahs elsewhere. The green dome marks the resting place of the Prophet ﷺ. Pray all your daily salahs here. The masjid is open 24/7 — if you can't sleep, come pray Tahajjud in this blessed place. Shoes off before entering, enter right foot first reciting dua.
📍 MAP ↗
🌹
Al-Rawdah al-Sharifa · الروضة الشريفة
The blessed garden between the Prophet's ﷺ pulpit (Minbar) and his chamber (grave) — marked by green carpets while the rest is red. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Between my house and my minbar is a garden from the gardens of Paradise" (Bukhari). Pray 2 Rakats here and make sincere dua. Very crowded — go after Fajr or in the middle of the night. Women also have designated times.
🌟
Grave of the Prophet ﷺ · الحجرة النبوية
The resting place of our beloved Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, and beside him Abu Bakr (RA) and Umar (RA). Stand at the golden grille, lower your voice, and say: As-salamu alaika ya Rasulallah, as-salamu alaika ya Nabiyyallah... The Prophet ﷺ hears all salams sent to him. Then step slightly right to send salaam to Abu Bakr (RA), then another step right for Umar (RA). Maintain complete adab (respect) here.
🎤
Minbar (Pulpit) · المنبر
The Prophet's ﷺ original pulpit from which he delivered khutbahs (sermons). The Prophet ﷺ said: "My minbar is on my hawd (pool in Jannah)". Touch or pray near the original location (marked today) — scholars recommend praying 2 Rakats and making dua near the minbar, as it is also described as close to a garden of Paradise.
🏛️
Ustuwanah al-Hannanah · اسطوانة الحنانة
The "Crying Pillar" — one of the original pillars of the Prophet's ﷺ mosque. The Prophet ﷺ used to lean against this tree trunk when giving Khutbah. When the minbar was built and he moved to it, the companions heard the trunk weep — and the Prophet ﷺ descended and hugged it. Pray near this pillar and remember your connection to the Prophet ﷺ.
🕌 MASAJID & ZIYARAT IN MADINAH
🪦
Jannat al-Baqi · جنة البقيع
The blessed cemetery right next to Masjid Nabawi — resting place of over 10,000 Companions. Buried here: Uthman ibn Affan (RA), Ibrahim (the Prophet's ﷺ son), wives of the Prophet ﷺ (Ummahat al-Mu'minin), Fatimah (RA), Hasan ibn Ali (RA), and countless great Companions. Open for men after Fajr and after Asr. Women: restricted but visible from outside. Make salaam: As-salamu alaykum ya ahlal quboor...
📍 MAP ↗
🕌
Masjid Quba · مسجد قباء
The first mosque ever built in Islam — built by the Prophet ﷺ himself upon his arrival in Madinah in 1 AH. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Praying 2 Rakats in Masjid Quba equals the reward of one Umrah" (Ibn Majah, Tirmidhi). He used to visit every Saturday. A must-visit — even if you only pray 2 Rakats and leave, you've earned an Umrah's reward! Located ~4km south of Masjid Nabawi.
📍 MAP ↗
⚔️
Jabal Uhud & Shuhada Graves · جبل أحد
The site of the Battle of Uhud (3 AH / 625 CE) — where 70 Companions were martyred. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Uhud is a mountain that loves us and we love it". Visit the graves of the Shuhada (martyrs) including Hamza ibn Abdul Muttalib (RA) — the "Chief of Martyrs" and the Prophet's ﷺ uncle. Make salaam and dua. Recite Surah Fatiha for each. The Prophet ﷺ used to visit and weep here. ~5km north of Masjid Nabawi.
📍 MAP ↗
🧭
Masjid Qiblatain · مسجد القبلتين
The mosque of two Qiblas — the unique place where the Qibla was changed by divine command mid-prayer from Jerusalem to Makkah (Quran 2:144). The Prophet ﷺ was leading Asr prayer facing Jerusalem when the verse was revealed — he immediately turned toward the Kaaba, and so did all the Companions behind him. Visit and pray 2 Rakats facing Makkah — a powerful connection to Islamic history. ~5km NW of Masjid Nabawi.
📍 MAP ↗
🛡️
Masjid al-Fath & Khandaq (Trench) · مسجد الفتح
Built at the site of the Battle of Khandaq (the Trench, 5 AH) — where the Prophet ﷺ made dua for 3 days and received the glad tiding of victory over the Romans, Persians, and Yemenis. The Prophet ﷺ's dua was answered here. A cluster of small mosques on the hillside — including Masjid Salman al-Farisi (RA), Masjid Abu Bakr (RA), Masjid Umar (RA), and Masjid Ali (RA). ~3.5km NW of Nabawi.
📍 MAP ↗
🌿
Masjid al-Ghamama · مسجد الغمامة
The mosque where the Prophet ﷺ prayed Eid salah and Salatul Istisqa (rain prayer) — and a cloud (ghamama) miraculously shaded him. One of the oldest mosques of Islam. Located ~500m south-west of Masjid Nabawi. A peaceful, uncrowded mosque — pray 2 Rakats and reflect on the miracles of the Prophet ﷺ.
📍 MAP ↗
🌴
Masjid Abu Bakr al-Siddiq RA · مسجد أبي بكر
One of the cluster of historic masajid near Masjid al-Fath — built at the spot where Abu Bakr (RA) prayed during the Battle of Khandaq. A beautiful small mosque dedicated to the greatest Companion. Pray here and send salawat on Abu Bakr (RA) who loved the Prophet ﷺ more than any other.
📍 MAP ↗
📚
Masjid Ali ibn Abi Talib RA
Located in the Khandaq cluster near Masjid al-Fath — the spot where Ali (RA) prayed during the battle. Also visit Masjid Umar (RA) nearby. These masajid together paint a complete picture of the blessed Companions who defended Islam during the Khandaq battle. Each is small, peaceful, and often uncrowded — a genuine spiritual experience.
🌊
Masjid al-Ijabah · مسجد الإجابة
The mosque of the "Answered Dua" — built at the spot where the Prophet ﷺ made 3 duas for his Ummah. Two were granted and one was withheld as mercy. It is narrated that whoever prays 2 Rakats here with sincerity, their dua will be answered. Make it a point to visit and pour your heart out in this masjid. Located ~2km NE of Masjid Nabawi.
📍 MAP ↗
🏔️
Jabal al-Rumah (Archers' Hill) · جبل الرماة
The hill at Uhud where the Prophet ﷺ stationed 50 archers under Abdullah ibn Jubayr (RA) with strict orders not to leave their post. When some descended thinking the battle was won, the Qurayshi cavalry counter-attacked from behind — leading to the reversal at Uhud. A profound reminder: obedience to the Prophet ﷺ is everything. Climb the hill and recite Quran and dua.
📍 MAP ↗
🌙
Wadi al-Aqiq · وادي العقيق
A blessed valley west of Madinah mentioned in hadith — the Prophet ﷺ said a voice came to him saying: "Pray in this blessed valley and say 'Umratun wa Hajjatun fi Hajjah'". Also historically the Miqat location for people of Madinah. Drive through for barakah and recite istighfar abundantly.
🕯️
Masjid al-Miqat / Dhul Hulayfah · مسجد الميقات
Also known as Abyar Ali — the Miqat for people of Madinah and those passing through it. The Prophet ﷺ put on Ihram for Hajj and Umrah from here. If you are travelling from Madinah to Makkah for Umrah, wear your Ihram and make your niyyah from this masjid. Located ~12km south-west of Masjid Nabawi near the airport road.

Labbayka Allahumma 'umrah
"Here I am O Allah, for Umrah." — Recite once at Miqat to enter Ihram. Then recite Talbiyah continuously.

"Here I am O Allah, here I am. Here I am, You have no partner, here I am. Verily all praise, grace and sovereignty belong to You. You have no partner." — Recite loudly (men) from Miqat until first sight of Kaaba.

"O Allah, increase this House in honour, greatness, nobility and reverence, and increase those who honour and respect it — those who perform Hajj or Umrah — in honour, nobility and righteousness." — Stop Talbiyah now, raise hands and make dua.

Bismillah · Allahu Akbar
"In the name of Allah, Allah is the Greatest." — Say this at the Black Stone before each of the 7 circuits. Kiss it, or touch it, or face it and point with right hand.

"Our Lord, grant us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire." — Quran 2:201. Recite this in the last stretch before the Black Stone each circuit.

"And take the Station of Ibrahim as a place of prayer." — Quran 2:125. Recite this, then pray 2 Rakats at Maqam Ibrahim. Read Surah Kafirun in 1st rakat, Surah Ikhlas in 2nd.

🕌 MASJID AL-HARAM — GATE GUIDE
KING ABDULAZIZ GATE (BAB MALIK)
NORTH-WEST
Main entrance for pilgrims from Ajyad hotel district. Zam-Zam Tower (Makkah Clock Tower) is directly North. Enter right foot first. This gate opens directly toward the Hateem side of Kaaba — ideal entry point for Tawaf.
KING FAHD GATE (BAB AL-MALIK FAHD)
NORTH · MAIN GATE
Largest and most prominent gate with the iconic minarets. Faces toward Makkah Clock Tower hotels. Best entry if coming from Abraj Al-Bait (Zam-Zam Tower) — walk straight south ~3 min.
BAB AS-SALAM (GATE OF PEACE)
NORTH-EAST · HISTORIC
Historic gate where the Prophet ﷺ entered. Recommended entry for Sunnah. Located near the Black Stone corner of Kaaba — ideal if starting Tawaf immediately. Say dua of entering Masjid here.
BAB IBRAHIM (IBRAHIM GATE)
SOUTH-WEST
Located near Maqam Ibrahim on the South-West side. Best if coming from Marwah / Sa'i corridor direction. After 2 Rakats at Maqam Ibrahim, exit from nearby gate toward Zamzam well.
BAB ALI / UMRAH GATE
EAST · SAFA SIDE
On the eastern side, leads directly toward Safa Hill. Best gate to use after completing Tawaf + 2 Rakats, heading to start Sa'i. The Mas'aa (Sa'i corridor) entrance is right outside this gate.
🧭 STEP-BY-STEP IN MASJID AL-HARAM
STEP 1 → ENTER HARAM · BAB AS-SALAM (NORTH-EAST)
Enter right foot first, recite dua of entering masjid. Walk straight South-West toward the open mataf (Tawaf area). You will see the Kaaba ahead. Stop at the Hajar al-Aswad (Black Stone) corner — it is on the eastern corner of the Kaaba, at ground level.
STEP 2 → TAWAF · KEEP KAABA ON YOUR LEFT · COUNTER-CLOCKWISE
Start at Black Stone corner (East side). Walk counter-clockwise (anti-clockwise): your first move is toward the North (past Hijr Ismail / Hateem), then West, then South, then East back to start. Each full circuit = 1 round. Complete 7 rounds. The green lights indicate Raml (brisk walking) section for men — in the innermost track only.
STEP 3 → MAQAM IBRAHIM · BEHIND IT · FACING KAABA
After 7 circuits, walk North-East away from Kaaba until you reach Maqam Ibrahim (a small golden enclosure). Stand behind it so Maqam Ibrahim is between you and Kaaba. Pray 2 Rakats. Then go East to the Zamzam area (inside/underground). Drink facing Qibla (facing Kaaba = West).
STEP 4 → SAFA HILL · EXIT EAST SIDE OF HARAM
Walk East out of the Haram through Bab Ali/Bab Safa. The Mas'aa (Sa'i corridor) is attached to the eastern wall of Haram. Enter the Mas'aa and go South toward Safa Hill (marked in green). Safa → Marwah direction = you walk North. Marwah → Safa = you walk South. End at Marwah (North end).
STEP 5 → HALQ/TAQSIR · RETURN TO HOTEL
After finishing at Marwah, exit the Mas'aa and find a barbershop (they are immediately outside Haram on all sides — row of shops on King Abdulaziz Road). Men: shave or trim all hair. Women: trim fingertip length privately. You are now out of Ihram — Umrah is complete! Inshallah Maqbool.

PRACTICAL GUIDANCE
TIPS & EMERGENCY SOS
🆘 EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
🔴 IF LOST IN HARAM
Stay calm · Go to the nearest Information desk (green signs) · Announce at Information Center Gate 1 or 79 · Call hotel/group leader · Emergency: ☎️ 911
🔴 IF FAMILY MEMBER IS LOST
Establish a meeting point at the hotel BEFORE entering Haram · Share WhatsApp live location · Lost persons often found at Information Center or hotel lobby · Women's section for female family members
🚨 MEDICAL EMERGENCY
Red Crescent stations inside Haram every 200m · Ajyad Emergency Hospital (near Haram): +966 12 556 6666 · Haram Medical Center (Gate 3) · Ambulance: 911
💡 PRE-TRIP SAFETY TIP
Write your hotel name, phone number, and room on a paper inside all clothing. Use an ID bracelet for elderly and children.
🌡️
Heat
Carry umbrella + small spray bottle. Stay hydrated with Zamzam. Visit Haram at night to avoid extreme heat.
🕰️
Best Times in Ramadan
Do Tawaf after Taraweeh (post midnight) — fewest crowds. Avoid Jumu'ah peak hours. Fajr time is magical — still and peaceful inside Haram.
📍
Accommodation
Stay within 500m of Haram for easy walking. Higher floors = farther from crowd noise.
👟
Footwear
Use slip-on sandals. Leave at shoe storage area outside Haram. Mark clearly with name. Floor gets very hot outside — wear socks.
📱
SIM Card
Buy Mobily, STC, or Zain SIM at Jeddah airport. Get 30-day data plan for maps and calling.
💱
Currency
Saudi Riyal (SAR). ATMs everywhere near Haram. Exchange rate ~22 INR = 1 SAR (approx).
🍽️
Iftar at Haram
Free Iftar spread inside Haram — arrive 30 min early. Dates + Zamzam first. Indian restaurants in Ajyad area open all night for Sehri too!
♿
Wheelchair
Free wheelchair service at all Haram gates. Book in advance for electric scooter on Haram website. Elderly-priority lanes available.
